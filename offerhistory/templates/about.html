About Car Deal Tracker

Why did I create this site?

I'm Jessica Lake and this is Car Deal Tracker.

This website is new but the story goes way back.

A number of years ago I bought my very first brand new vehicle. It was a bright white 2013 Ford Edge. I bought it in 2014 and negotiated a good deal, or so I thought.

When I sat down with a glass of wine to celebrate and started reviewing my signed papers I realized that I had been “tricked” into paying a lot more than I originally agreed.

Now it's my personal mission to make sure this does not happen to you.

This is exactly what happened. Note I've rounded the numbers for readability.

Purchase Terms

The sticker price on the car was $45,000. We agreed that I would pay $36,000 financed over 72 months at 0.99%.

Trade In

I agreed to trade in a Honda Civic for $10,000. I could have fetched more selling privately but I was willing to do it for the convenience and extra GST savings.

Agreement

It seemed straightforward - I'd be financing $26,000 plus tax for 6 years at .99%.

What happened?

I should have never signed on the dotted line. The finance manager was SLICK and I couldn't comprehend the impact of some of these charges he slipped in. Especially over 72 months of bi-weekly payments.

  1. The finance manager indicated the trade in value was after tax (there is 13% sales tax in Manitoba) so I received the equivalent of about $8800 for the trade in. The salesman never mentioned this.

  1. I still don't know exactly what magic the finance manager pulled but the $9000 off sticker I thought I negotiated was a rebate that I had to pay tax on. There was no earlier mention of this.

  1. The 0.99% financing was only available for 5 year loans so I had to pay 1.99%. This was an “honest” mistake.

  1. I am embarrassed to admit this but I agreed to an extended warranty despite the fact that I only drive 8,000 to 10,000 kms per year. I bought this, I should not have.

  1. And the worst part is the “mandatory tire insurance and glass etching” I bought for $800.I threatened to walk out, he said he understood but that it was mandatory so he couldn't do anything about it. Guess what? I didn't walk out.

I was excited about my new ride but when I got home figured out exactly what I signed I was embarrassed and sick to my stomach.

So now what?

I am here to educate you on how to get the best deal possible on a car.

Unfortunately, it is complicated and there is a lot more to it than agreeing on a price with your salesperson.

Car Deal Tracker

Tracks manufacturer incentives (also called offers, rebates, discounts). This is important because it's by far the easiest way to save a significant amount of money on your next vehicle purchase.

Car Deal Tracker will let you know if the current dealer incentives are competitive with what has been offered in the past. I also have all sorts of buying guides and tips that will prepare you to deal with the salesperson.

Most importantly, I will also arm you with all the information you need to ensure you don't get taken advantage of in the finance managers office.
